SECTION 1 — ACCOUNT OPENING & MANAGEMENT
================================================================

1.1 How do I open a new bank account?
To open a new account with First National Bank, you will need to
visit any of our branches or apply online at fnb.com/open-account.

Required documents:
  • Government-issued photo ID (Passport, Driver's License, or
    National ID card)
  • Proof of address dated within the last 3 months
    (utility bill, bank statement, or lease agreement)
  • PAN Card (for Indian residents) or Tax Identification Number
  • Initial deposit (minimum ₹1,000 for Savings, ₹10,000 for
    Current accounts)

Processing time: Online applications are approved within 24 hours.
Branch applications are processed on the same working day.

1.2 What types of accounts does First National Bank offer?
  • Savings Account — For individuals; earns 3.5% p.a. interest
  • Current Account — For businesses; no transaction limits
  • Salary Account — Zero minimum balance for salaried individuals
  • Student Account — For students aged 18–25; zero fees
  • Joint Account — Held by two or more individuals
  • Senior Citizen Account — For customers aged 60+; 0.5% extra
    interest and priority service

1.3 What is the minimum balance requirement?
  • Regular Savings Account: ₹5,000 monthly average balance
  • Premium Savings Account: ₹25,000 monthly average balance
  • Salary Account: No minimum balance required
  • Current Account: ₹10,000 monthly average balance
  • Student Account: No minimum balance required

Failure to maintain minimum balance will incur a charge of ₹200
per month (waived for Salary and Student accounts).

1.4 How do I update my personal information?
You can update your address, phone number, email, or nominee
details through:
  • Internet Banking → Profile → Update Details
  • Mobile App → Settings → Personal Information
  • Branch visit with supporting documents
  • Customer care: 1800-XXX-XXXX (toll-free, 24x7)

SECTION 2 — HOME LOANS & MORTGAGES
================================================================

2.1 What are the eligibility criteria for a home loan?
First National Bank offers home loans to customers meeting the
following eligibility criteria:

Age:
  • Salaried individuals: 21 to 60 years at loan maturity
  • Self-employed individuals: 21 to 65 years at loan maturity

Income requirements:
  • Salaried: Minimum gross monthly income of ₹25,000
  • Self-employed: Minimum net annual income of ₹3,00,000 (ITR)

Employment/Business stability:
  • Salaried: Minimum 2 years of total work experience with
    at least 1 year with the current employer
  • Self-employed professionals: Minimum 3 years in current
    profession or business

Credit score:
  • Minimum CIBIL score of 700 required
  • Scores above 750 qualify for preferential interest rates

Property requirements:
  • Property must be legally clear and free from disputes
  • Located within approved municipal limits
  • Age of property not more than 40 years for resale properties

2.2 What documents are required for a home loan?
Identity & Address Proof (any one each):
  • Passport, Aadhaar Card, Voter ID, Driver's License

Income Documents — Salaried:
  • Last 3 months salary slips
  • Form 16 for last 2 years
  • Last 6 months bank statements
  • Employment letter / appointment letter

Income Documents — Self-employed:
  • ITR with computation for last 2 years
  • Audited balance sheet and P&L for last 2 years
  • Last 12 months business bank statements
  • Business registration certificate

Property Documents:
  • Sale agreement / allotment letter
  • Title deeds for last 30 years
  • Approved building plan and NOC from builder
  • Property tax receipt

2.3 What is the interest rate on a home loan?
Current home loan interest rates (as of January 2026):
  • Floating rate: 8.50% – 9.75% p.a. (linked to REPO rate)
  • Fixed rate: 9.25% – 10.50% p.a. (fixed for first 3 years)

Rate slabs by loan amount:
  • Up to ₹30 lakh: 8.50% (CIBIL ≥ 750)
  • ₹30 lakh to ₹75 lakh: 8.75%
  • Above ₹75 lakh: 9.00%
  • Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (PMAY) subsidy: Additional 0.25%
    reduction for eligible first-time home buyers

2.4 What is the maximum home loan tenure?
  • Maximum tenure: 30 years (360 months)
  • Minimum tenure: 5 years (60 months)
  • Loan must be repaid before the borrower turns 70 years of age

2.5 How much home loan can I get?
Loan-to-Value (LTV) ratio:
  • Up to ₹30 lakh: 90% of property value
  • ₹30 lakh to ₹75 lakh: 80% of property value
  • Above ₹75 lakh: 75% of property value

Maximum loan amount: ₹5 Crore for standard home loans.
Premium home loans up to ₹10 Crore available for HNI customers.

2.6 How long does home loan approval take?
  • In-principle approval: Within 48 hours of application
  • Final sanction: 5–7 working days after document submission
  • Disbursement: 3–5 working days after property verification

SECTION 4 — CREDIT CARDS
================================================================

4.1 How do I apply for a credit card?
You can apply for a First National Bank credit card:
  • Online: fnb.com/credit-cards
  • Mobile App: Cards → Apply for Credit Card
  • Branch: Visit any branch with required documents
  • Pre-approved offer: Check eligibility via NetBanking

Eligibility:
  • Age: 21 to 65 years
  • Minimum annual income:
    - Classic Card: ₹3,00,000 p.a.
    - Gold Card: ₹5,00,000 p.a.
    - Platinum Card: ₹8,00,000 p.a.
    - Signature Card: ₹15,00,000 p.a.
  • Good credit history (CIBIL score ≥ 700)
  • No defaults or write-offs in the last 3 years

4.2 How can I increase my credit card limit?
You can request a credit limit increase through:
  • Mobile App → Cards → Credit Limit Enhancement
  • NetBanking → Credit Cards → Manage → Limit Enhancement
  • Call customer care: 1800-XXX-XXXX
  • Branch visit

Eligibility for limit increase:
  • Account must be active for at least 6 months
  • No overdue payments in the last 12 months
  • Utilisation should be below 80% consistently
  • Income increase (proof may be required)
  • CIBIL score above 740

The bank reviews limit increase requests within 2–5 working days.
Limit increases are granted at the bank's discretion.

4.3 What are the credit card charges?
  • Annual fee:
    - Classic: ₹500 (waived if annual spend > ₹50,000)
    - Gold: ₹1,000 (waived if annual spend > ₹1,00,000)
    - Platinum: ₹2,500 (waived if annual spend > ₹3,00,000)
    - Signature: ₹5,000 (waived if annual spend > ₹10,00,000)
  • Finance charges: 3.5% per month (42% p.a.) on outstanding
  • Late payment fee: ₹100 to ₹1,200 depending on outstanding
  • Cash advance fee: 2.5% of amount (minimum ₹500)
  • Overlimit fee: ₹500
  • Foreign transaction fee: 3.5% + applicable GST

4.4 What is the billing cycle and payment due date?
  • Statement is generated on the 15th of every month
  • Payment due date is the 5th of the following month
  • Minimum payment due: 5% of total outstanding or ₹200
    (whichever is higher)
  • Grace period: 20 days from statement date

SECTION 7 — TRANSACTION DISPUTES & FRAUD
================================================================

7.1 How do I dispute a transaction on my account?
If you notice an unauthorized or incorrect transaction:

Step 1 — Report immediately:
  • Call 1800-XXX-XXXX (24x7 toll-free)
  • Or use Mobile App → Help → Report Fraud
  • Or visit any branch with your account details

Step 2 — Block your card (if card fraud):
  • Mobile App → Cards → Block Card
  • Or call the helpline immediately

Step 3 — Submit a written dispute:
  • Fill out the Transaction Dispute Form (available on our website
    or at any branch)
  • Attach supporting documents (SMS alerts, receipts, screenshots)
  • Submit within 30 days of the transaction date

Step 4 — Investigation and resolution:
  • Disputes are acknowledged within 24 hours
  • Resolution time: 7–45 working days depending on the type
    of dispute and card network (Visa/Mastercard/RuPay)
  • You will receive an SMS/email update at each stage
  • If the dispute is resolved in your favour, the amount is
    credited to your account within 5 working days

7.2 What if my card is lost or stolen?
  • Immediately block the card: App → Cards → Block Card
    OR call 1800-XXX-XXXX
  • Zero liability on unauthorized transactions reported within
    24 hours of discovery
  • Request a replacement card (delivered in 5–7 working days)
  • No fee for first replacement per year; ₹200 thereafter

7.3 RBI guidelines on fraud liability:
Per RBI circular, customer liability for unauthorized transactions:
  • Zero liability if reported within 3 working days of bank
    notification, provided there is no negligence by customer
  • Limited liability (up to ₹10,000) if reported within 4–7
    working days
  • Full liability if reported after 7 working days or if
    customer negligence is established

SECTION 9 — CLOSING AN ACCOUNT
================================================================

9.1 How do I close my account?
To close your First National Bank account:

Step 1 — Clear all dues:
  • Ensure zero outstanding balance on linked credit cards or
    loans associated with the account
  • Close or transfer any standing instructions / auto-debits

Step 2 — Visit a branch:
  • Bring original passbook, debit card(s), and cheque book
  • Carry your government-issued photo ID
  • Fill out the Account Closure Request Form

Step 3 — Receive remaining balance:
  • Balance can be received via banker's cheque or NEFT transfer
    to another bank account
  • Processing time: Same day for amounts up to ₹50,000;
    2–3 working days for larger amounts

Important:
  • Closure within 6 months of opening: ₹500 fee applies
  • After 6 months: No closure fee
  • Joint accounts require the signature of all holders for closure
  • Accounts with nominee registered: Nominee details on record

9.2 Can I freeze my account temporarily instead of closing it?
Yes. You can request a temporary account freeze if you are
travelling abroad or want to pause account activity:
  • Submit a written request at any branch
  • Duration: 1 month to 1 year (renewable)
  • No fee for freezing; ₹100 to unfreeze
  • UPI, NEFT, IMPS, and ATM will be disabled during freeze
